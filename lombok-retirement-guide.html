Visa Options for Retirees

Indonesia offers several visa pathways for foreign retirees. Here are the most relevant:

Retirement KITAS (ITAS 319)

This is the dedicated retirement visa for foreigners aged 55 and above. Key details:

  • Duration: 1 year, renewable annually (up to 5 years via KITAP)
  • Requirements: Proof of pension or savings ($1,500/month minimum), health insurance, rental agreement or property ownership, passport valid 18+ months
  • Cost: Approximately $1,200‚Äì$2,000/year including agent fees
  • Restrictions: Cannot work or run a business in Indonesia
  • Benefits: Multiple entry, KITAP (permanent stay) eligibility after 5 years

Second Home Visa (Visa Rumah Kedua)

Introduced in 2022, this newer visa option is designed for wealthy foreigners who want to live in Indonesia long-term:

  • Duration: 5 or 10 years
  • Requirements: Proof of $130,000+ in savings/investments, or property ownership in Indonesia
  • Cost: $2,000‚Äì$3,000 for the initial application
  • Benefits: Long validity, multiple entry, no age requirement, family members included

B211A Social/Cultural Visa

For those testing the waters before committing to retirement:

  • Duration: 60 days, extendable up to 180 days
  • Cost: $200‚Äì$350 including extensions
  • Best for: Trial stays, exploring areas, getting settled before applying for KITAS

Healthcare in Lombok

Healthcare is often the biggest concern for retirees considering Southeast Asia. Here's the honest picture for Lombok:

What's Available

  • Mataram General Hospital (RSUD Mataram): The island's largest public hospital with decent facilities for routine care and emergencies
  • Private clinics: Several international-standard clinics in Mataram and Senggigi offer GP consultations, dental care, and basic diagnostics. Expect to pay $20‚Äì$50 per visit
  • Pharmacies: Well-stocked and affordable. Many medications that require prescriptions elsewhere are available over the counter
  • Traditional medicine: Lombok has a rich tradition of herbal medicine and massage therapy ‚Äî wonderful for wellness, though not a substitute for modern care

For Serious Medical Needs

For specialist care, surgery, or complex procedures, most expats travel to:

  • Bali (25-minute flight): BIMC Hospital and Siloam Hospital offer international-standard care
  • Singapore (3-hour flight): World-class hospitals for major procedures
  • Jakarta (2-hour flight): Excellent specialist hospitals at a fraction of Singapore's cost

Health Insurance

We strongly recommend international health insurance for retirees. Popular options include:

  • Cigna Global: Comprehensive plans from $150‚Äì$400/month depending on age and coverage
  • Allianz Care: Good regional plans with Indonesia coverage
  • Pacific Cross: Popular Southeast Asia-focused insurer, more affordable at $80‚Äì$200/month
  • Local insurance (BPJS): Indonesia's national health program ‚Äî available to KITAS holders for ~$10/month, though quality varies

Many routine medical expenses are so affordable in Indonesia that retirees find their overall healthcare costs are a fraction of what they paid back home, even with private insurance.

Where to Live in Lombok

Lombok offers several distinct areas, each with its own character:

South Lombok (Kuta Area)

The fastest-growing area, home to the Mandalika development zone, world-class surf breaks, and stunning beaches like Tanjung Aan and Selong Belanak. This is where most new villa developments ‚Äî including Yara Estates ‚Äî are located. It's ideal for active retirees who love the beach, surfing, and being at the centre of Lombok's growth story.

Senggigi (West Coast)

The most established tourist area with the largest existing expat community. Close to Mataram (the capital) for shopping and healthcare. Sunsets over the Lombok Strait with Bali's Mount Agung in the distance. Good for retirees who prioritise convenience and social connections.

Gili Islands

The three Gili islands (Trawangan, Meno, Air) offer car-free island living. Gili Air is particularly popular with long-term expats and retirees seeking ultimate tranquility. Limited healthcare access is the main drawback ‚Äî a boat ride is required for anything beyond basic first aid.

Mataram

The capital city offers the most urban experience on the island ‚Äî malls, hospitals, restaurants, and services. Not the tropical paradise lifestyle most retirees envision, but practical for those who need regular medical access or prefer city amenities.

Practical Tips for Retiring in Lombok

  1. Do a trial run: Spend 2‚Äì3 months in Lombok on a social visa before committing. Rent in different areas to find your fit.
  2. Learn basic Bahasa Indonesia: Even a few phrases go enormously far. Locals appreciate the effort and it makes daily life much richer.
  3. Set up a local bank account: BCA and Bank Mandiri are the most foreigner-friendly. You'll need your KITAS or passport.
  4. Get a local SIM card: Telkomsel offers the best coverage on Lombok. Data plans are incredibly cheap ‚Äî $5‚Äì$10/month for generous allowances.
  5. Join online communities: Facebook groups like "Lombok Expats" and "Living in Lombok" are invaluable for advice, recommendations, and social connections.
  6. Hire a local helper: A household helper (cook/cleaner) costs $150‚Äì$250/month and dramatically improves quality of life. It also supports the local economy.
  7. Plan for the wet season: November‚ÄìMarch brings rain (usually afternoon showers, not all-day). Many retirees use this as their travel-home period.
  8. Keep important documents safe: Store copies of your passport, visa, and insurance digitally. The nearest embassy (Australian, in Mataram) can help in emergencies.
